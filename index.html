Responsive Design Report


Why Responsive Design is Important & Google Approved

In 2015, Google updated its search algorithms to prioritize websites that display well on mobile devices to improve user experience. Companies with websites only designed for a desktop experience, are likely losing out on leads and sales, and pushing users to a competitor's website. Responsive design is important because 60% of searches now come from mobile devices, and it facilitates:

  1. Reable text without zoom
  2. Space for tap targets
  3. No horizontal scrolling
  4. The best possible and consistent user experience to increase lead generation, sales & conversions
  5. Increase reach to prospective customers
  6. Analytics, tracking & reporting
  7. Reduced time & cost to manage website content, and
  8. A competitive advantage over competition without
Responsive design is accomplished taking advantage of three main components: fluid grids, flexible text & images, and media queries.

Powerful Examples of Responsive Design

All 11 examples of responsive design were excellent. The consitency and clean look in Shopify's website was an ideal design. Only the call-to-action button and illustrations changed from desktop to mobile devices. The button and illustrations moved from the right of the form field and copy, to below them.
